Looking back at the past 12 weeks, the First Cloud Journey (FCJ) program has truly been a massive stepping stone for a Software Engineering student like me. Here are some of my honest thoughts and feedback for the team:

### General Evaluation

**1. Working Environment & Culture**  
AWS offers an incredibly open environment. There is no strict hierarchical barrier, and the engineers are always willing to jump into a pairing session to help point out my mistakes. Even though the work was mostly remote/hybrid, the weekly sync-ups kept me feeling closely connected to the team.

**2. Mentor Support**  
My mentor was absolutely fantastic. Instead of spoon-feeding solutions or fixing bugs for me, he often asked reverse questions: "If the traffic scales by 10x, which service in this flow breaks first?". This approach forced me to think about system design rather than just writing code that 'works'.

**3. Alignment with Major**  
The Document Management System (DMS) project aligned perfectly with my Software Engineering major. At HUTECH, I learned a lot about software logic, and joining FCJ taught me how to deploy that software onto the Cloud following Enterprise standards.

### What I Appreciated Most
- Getting hands-on experience with real-world AWS services (especially the Serverless stack).
- The team's emphasis on Security from the design phase (Identity with Cognito, IAM Role principle of least privilege) rather than treating it as an afterthought.

### Constructive Suggestions for the Program
- The volume of knowledge in the first few weeks is quite heavy for students without a strong Cloud background. I think it would be helpful to provide cheat sheets or simple mini-labs in weeks 1-2 to help newcomers catch up more easily.
- Having a mid-term offline workshop for interns to meet and network in person would make the experience even better.

### Conclusion
If any of my friends ask, I will 100% recommend they apply for the next season of the FCJ program. A huge and sincere thank you to AWS Vietnam, the mentors, and the organizing committee for creating such a practical, challenging, yet incredibly rewarding playground!
